Key Impact Numbers

Here are some critical statistics highlighting the global challenges and progress related to SDG 16:

  • In 2024, loss of lives amid armed conflicts surged 40 per cent from 2023, marking the third consecutive annual rise.
  • About four times more children and women were killed in 2023â€“2024 than in the previous biennium; of these, 8 in 10 child deaths and 7 in 10 female deaths occurred in Gaza.
  • The number of forcibly displaced people reached an unprecedented 123.2 million by the end of 2024.
  • The global homicide rate declined by 5 per cent between 2015 and 2022 from 5.9 per 100,000 people in 2015 to 5.2 in 2023.
  • Globally, detected human trafficking victims increased by 25 per cent in 2022 compared to pre-pandemic levels and by 43 per cent compared to 2020. A key driver of this surge is the growing number of child victims, which has risen by 31 per cent since 2019.
  • Killings and disappearances of human rights defenders, journalists and trade unionists remained alarmingly high in 2024, with at least 502 killings documented across 44 countries and 123 disappearances documented across 37 countries.

Compelling Success Stories

100 Days Campaign of Government Accountability (Palestine)

Launched by the Palestinian Consultative Staff for Developing NGOs (PCS) and a coalition of 84 civil society organizations, this campaign aimed to bridge the gap between civil society and the government. It fostered active participation and increased partnership in community affairs and public resource management, leading to more institutionalized community accountability.

Impact: Created sustainable community accountability, increased government engagement, and highlighted the need for greater openness to civil society participation.

Empowering Indigenous Women to Access Information (Mexico)

ARTICLE 19 Mexico and El Colectivo Feminista la Casa de la Mujer Ixim Antsetic empowered Indigenous women in Chiapas to use the Freedom of Information Act. They taught women how to request relevant information about medical services, improving healthcare in their communities.

Impact: Empowered women to protect their communities and children, increased involvement in decision-making processes, and improved access to public interest information.

Localization Through Whole-of-Society Approach (Somaliland)

The Somaliland SDG16+ Coalition facilitated an extensive and consultative process to drive SDG16+ localization. This generated buy-in across civil society and national/local government, using a baseline report to measure progress and maintain focus on targets.

Impact: Supported and drove SDG16+ localization, fostering ownership and involvement from diverse stakeholders, and establishing a framework for measuring progress.

Living Portfolio

Impact: Reduced corruption perception index in several countries, leading to improved governance and public trust.

Focus: Advancing the rule of law worldwide through data, advocacy, and community engagement.

Impact: Provided critical data for policy-making, supported legal reforms, and empowered communities to demand justice.

Focus: Providing open-source platform for managing and publishing SDG data, enhancing transparency and accountability.

AI-Powered Insights and Recommendations

Insight 1: Interconnectedness of Peace and Development: Analysis shows a strong correlation between reductions in violence and improvements in economic stability and social cohesion. Regions with sustained peace-building efforts demonstrate higher rates of poverty reduction and educational attainment.

Recommendation: Prioritize integrated peace and development strategies, focusing on local ownership and community-led initiatives to ensure long-term sustainability.

Insight 2: Digitalization for Transparency: Countries adopting digital governance platforms for public services and financial oversight exhibit significantly lower corruption perception indices and higher citizen trust in institutions.

Recommendation: Invest in secure, accessible digital infrastructure for public administration, promoting e-governance and open data policies to enhance transparency and accountability.

Insight 3: Youth and Women as Agents of Change: Data indicates that programs empowering youth and women in justice and governance sectors lead to more inclusive and effective institutional reforms. Their participation is crucial for challenging existing power structures and advocating for equitable policies.

Recommendation: Scale up investments in leadership development and capacity building for young people and women, ensuring their meaningful participation in decision-making processes at all levels.
