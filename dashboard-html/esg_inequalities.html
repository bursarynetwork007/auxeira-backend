Ensuring Sustainable Consumption and Production Patterns

Driving circular economy, waste reduction, sustainable supply chains, and resource efficiency.

Key Impact Numbers

In 2022, global food waste reached 1.05 billion metric tons, with 60% from households. An estimated 1 billion meals are wasted daily. Food loss and waste generate 8 to 10 percent of greenhouse gas emissions.

E-waste generation rose from 6.3 to 7.8 kilograms per capita from 2015 to 2022, totaling 62 million metric tons in 2022. Only 1.7 kilograms per capita was collected and managed in an environmentally sound manner. By 2030, e-waste generation is projected to increase to 10 kilograms per capita per year.

Domestic material consumption grew by 69 percent from 2000 to 2022, reaching 96.0 billion metric tons. The global material footprint increased by 71 percent from 2000 to 2022, reaching 98.0 billion metric tons.

Compelling Success Stories

Beyond the Bag

A collaborative effort between direct competitors to drive customers away from single-use bags, accelerating innovation in reusable packaging solutions.

PALPA (Finland)

Collaboration that led to Finland's successful deposit return system for beverage containers, demonstrating how competitors can collectively drive market change.

HolyGrail 2.0

Uniting the value chain to advance sorting technology for plastic waste, addressing a bottleneck in post-consumer recycling.

ZEMBA (Zero Emission Maritime Buyers Alliance)

A collective procurement initiative by buyers to unlock zero-emission shipping, showcasing efforts towards sustainable supply chains.

New Investment Opportunities

  • Zero-Waste Packaging-as-a-Service: Investment in companies developing reusable, automated, and shape-adjusting packaging solutions for online retailers, reducing waste and shipping costs.
  • Circular Battery Energy Storage Systems: Opportunities in startups repurposing retired EV batteries into BESS, maximizing existing potential and reducing the need for new battery production.
  • Industrial Carbon Upcycling: Investment in companies that upcycle industrial carbon emissions into sustainable fuels and chemical feedstocks, enabling carbon-negative supply chains.
  • AI-based Food Waste Prediction: Opportunities in startups developing AI-powered prediction apps for businesses to sell surplus food, leading to better waste management and reduced greenhouse gas emissions.

AI-Powered Insights and Recommendations

Insights from Data and Trends:

  • Persistent Waste Challenges: Global food waste (1.05 billion metric tons in 2022) and e-waste (7.8 kg per capita in 2022, projected to reach 10 kg by 2030) remain significant issues, indicating a gap between policy and effective implementation.
  • Resource Consumption Growth: Domestic material consumption and global material footprint continue to grow, highlighting the ongoing challenge of decoupling economic growth from resource depletion.
  • Regional Disparities: Significant regional inequalities exist in material consumption and e-waste management, particularly in lower-income countries, necessitating equitable solutions.
  • Circular Economy Momentum: The rise of innovative circular economy startups and successful case studies demonstrates a growing market and technological capacity for sustainable practices.
  • Investment Potential: Diverse investment opportunities exist in circular economy solutions, indicating a fertile ground for impact investing aligned with SDG 12.

AI-Powered Recommendations:

  • Predictive Waste Management Systems: Deploy AI-powered predictive analytics to optimize waste collection, forecast generation patterns, and identify intervention areas.
  • Material Flow Optimization with AI: Implement AI algorithms to map and optimize material flows within industrial ecosystems, enhancing resource efficiency.
  • Personalized Sustainable Consumption Nudges: Utilize AI to develop personalized recommendations for consumers to adopt more sustainable consumption patterns.
  • AI-driven Supply Chain Transparency and Optimization: Employ AI and blockchain to enhance transparency, identify risks, and optimize sustainable sourcing and production.
  • Automated Waste Sorting and Recycling: Invest in AI-powered robotics and computer vision for advanced waste sorting, increasing material purity and recycling effectiveness.
