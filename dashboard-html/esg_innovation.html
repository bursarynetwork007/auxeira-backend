Driving Equality, Fostering Inclusion

Globally, over half of 108 countries achieved higher income growth among the bottom 40% than the national average. However, 12% of the population still lives below 50% of median income, and the global refugee population has surged to 37.8 million.

Compelling Success Stories

Compelling Success Stories

1. Bariki Women SACCO & Kwara: Empowering Financial Inclusion in Kenya Bariki Women SACCO, a leading cooperative in Kenya, partnered with Kwara, a digital banking platform, to revolutionize its operations and significantly enhance financial inclusion for its members. This collaboration led to the SACCO winning the prestigious ‘Best Women Cooperative prize’.
  • Impact: Kwara's core banking platform enabled seamless tracking of over 10,000 members' savings and loan repayments. The 'Haraka loan' feature, allowing instant loan access via a simple USSD code (*341#), has provided crucial financial support during emergencies, fostering financial independence among women. The digital transformation also extended the SACCO's reach, allowing members in remote areas and even abroad (Qatar, UAE) to save and borrow effortlessly, breaking geographical barriers to financial services. This shift to a paperless, automated system drastically improved operational efficiency and member trust.
  • Metrics: Over 10,000 members served; instant loan access; expanded geographical reach; significant increase in member trust and operational efficiency.
2. Burkina Faso: Progressive Fiscal Policies for Income Equality Burkina Faso implemented targeted government policies to address income disparities, demonstrating how legislative action can drive significant reductions in inequality.
  • Impact: The government increased the national minimum wage, directly benefiting low-income workers and improving their purchasing power. Concurrently, reforms were introduced to make the value-added tax (VAT) system more progressive, ensuring that wealthier individuals contributed a larger share, thereby redistributing wealth and reducing the burden on lower-income households.
  • Metrics: Increased national minimum wage; more progressive VAT system; direct positive impact on income distribution for lower-income populations.
3. Paraguay: Strengthening Social Protection and Formal Employment Paraguay undertook comprehensive measures to enhance social protection and formalize its labor market, leading to improved economic equality and opportunities.
  • Impact: The government significantly increased the share of formal employment, providing workers with better job security, benefits, and legal protections. This was coupled with a substantial doubling of the minimum wage, which uplifted many families out of poverty. Furthermore, increased expenditure on public health improved access to essential healthcare services for all citizens, particularly the most vulnerable, reducing health-related inequalities.
  • Metrics: Doubled minimum wage; increased formal employment rates; higher public health expenditure; improved access to healthcare services.

Living Portfolio: Companies Making a Difference

Living Portfolio: Companies Making a Difference

1. Mastercard: Empowering Financial Identity and Inclusion Mastercard is actively working to reduce economic inequality by leveraging the "power of identity." Their mission focuses on creating solutions that enable partners to extend financial services and inclusion to underserved populations globally.
  • Initiatives: Mastercard partners with governments and NGOs to provide official personal identification, particularly for women, who often lack formal identity documents. A key example is their collaboration with UN Women in Nigeria, where they provided half a million Nigerian women with National ID cards that double as electronic payment cards. They also work with Mercy Corps to register 18,000 Nigerian girls and women for eID cards, granting them access to their first formal identity.
  • Impact: By providing formal identification and electronic payment solutions, Mastercard facilitates access to government benefits, financial services, and greater economic agency, directly contributing to social and economic inclusion and reducing inequality.
2. Procter & Gamble (P&G): Promoting Supplier Diversity and Economic Opportunity P&G demonstrates a strong commitment to reducing inequality through its comprehensive Supplier Diversity program, which creates economic opportunities for underrepresented businesses.
  • Initiatives: Since 1976, P&G’s program in the United States has focused on creating opportunities for minority-owned, women-owned, LGBT+, veteran, and disability-owned businesses. The program has expanded globally, with partnerships with organizations like UN Women and WeConnect to support women entrepreneurs worldwide. P&G spends over $2 billion annually with diverse suppliers in the US, making it a member of the Billion Dollar Roundtable.
  • Impact: This initiative fosters economic growth and reduces disparities by integrating diverse businesses into P&G’s supply chain, providing them with significant economic opportunities and contributing to a more inclusive economy.
3. McDonald’s: Investing in Employee Well-being and Education McDonald’s is committed to reducing inequality by investing in the financial and educational well-being of its employees, particularly those in company-owned restaurants.
  • Initiatives: In the US, McDonald’s has enhanced employee benefits, offering substantial wage increases and paid time-off. Approximately 90,000 employees in company-owned restaurants are eligible for these benefits. Through its Archways to Education initiative, McDonald’s provides free high school completion and college tuition assistance to around 750,000 employees (both company-owned and franchised). Additionally, non-native English speakers can access free English language classes.
  • Impact: These programs directly address economic inequality by improving employee income, providing access to education and skill development, and enhancing overall well-being, thereby creating pathways for upward mobility and reducing disparities in opportunity.

New Investment Opportunities

New Investment Opportunities

1. Inclusive Digital Financial Services for Underserved Markets
  • Opportunity: Investment in companies developing and deploying digital banking platforms, mobile money solutions, and microfinance technologies specifically designed for populations traditionally excluded from mainstream financial services. This includes solutions for rural communities, low-income individuals, and women in developing economies.
  • Rationale: Financial exclusion is a significant driver of inequality, limiting economic participation and upward mobility. Digital financial services can dramatically lower barriers to access, enabling individuals to save, borrow, and manage their finances more effectively. This fosters economic independence, supports small businesses, and reduces the cost of remittances.
  • Potential Impact: Increased access to credit for micro-entrepreneurs, higher savings rates among low-bottom 40% income households, reduced transaction costs for migrant remittances (target of less than 3%), and improved financial literacy and resilience for millions.
2. Technology for Accessible Education and Healthcare
  • Opportunity: Investment in innovative technology companies that are bridging gaps in access to quality education and healthcare for marginalized communities. This includes platforms for remote learning, AI-powered diagnostic tools for underserved regions, and telehealth solutions that reach populations with limited infrastructure.
  • Rationale: Disparities in access to education and healthcare perpetuate cycles of inequality. Technology can democratize access to these fundamental services, providing quality learning opportunities and essential medical care to those who need it most, irrespective of their location or socioeconomic status.
  • Potential Impact: Improved educational outcomes for children in remote areas, reduced health disparities through early diagnosis and remote consultations, enhanced skill development for vulnerable populations, and a more equitable distribution of essential services.
3. Ethical Supply Chains and Fair Labor Practices
  • Opportunity: Investment in businesses that prioritize and implement ethical supply chain management and fair labor practices. This includes companies that ensure fair wages, safe working conditions, eliminate child labor, and promote gender equality and non-discrimination throughout their global operations.
  • Rationale: Unethical labor practices and exploitative supply chains exacerbate global inequality. Investing in companies committed to fair labor not only improves the lives of workers but also drives systemic change towards more equitable economic systems. This aligns with SDG 10 targets on promoting universal social, economic, and political inclusion and ensuring equal opportunity.
  • Potential Impact: Enhanced worker well-being and livelihoods, reduction in exploitative labor practices, increased economic empowerment for vulnerable workers, and a more just and sustainable global economy. This includes companies that ensure fair wages, safe working conditions, eliminate child labor, and promote gender equality and non-discrimination throughout their global operations. economy.

AI-Powered Insights and Recommendations

AI-Powered Insights and Recommendations

Insights from Current Trends and Progress: Analysis of recent data reveals a mixed landscape for SDG 10. While some progress has been made in pro-poor income growth in over half of the surveyed countries, significant challenges persist. The global labor income share in GDP is declining, exacerbating inequality, and the global refugee population has more than doubled since 2015, highlighting growing humanitarian and social inclusion crises. Furthermore, the rising cost of remittances disproportionately affects migrant workers and their families, hindering financial inclusion efforts. Insights from Success Stories and Portfolio Companies: Success stories like Bariki Women SACCO demonstrate the transformative power of digital financial services in empowering marginalized communities, particularly women, by providing accessible and efficient financial tools. Government policies in Burkina Faso and Paraguay highlight the critical role of progressive fiscal measures, minimum wage increases, and investments in social protection and formal employment in reducing inequality. Companies like Mastercard and P&G illustrate how private sector initiatives, such as promoting financial identity and supplier diversity, can drive economic inclusion and create opportunities for underrepresented groups. Recommendations for Accelerated Progress:
  1. Prioritize Inclusive Digital Financial Solutions: Invest in and scale digital platforms that cater to the financially excluded, especially in rural areas and for vulnerable populations. This includes supporting mobile banking, microfinance, and accessible payment systems to foster economic independence and reduce remittance costs.
  2. Strengthen Progressive Fiscal and Social Policies: Advocate for and implement government policies that ensure equitable wealth distribution, such as progressive taxation, robust social protection programs, and fair minimum wage policies. These measures are crucial for addressing systemic inequalities and fostering inclusive growth.
  3. Promote Ethical Supply Chains and Fair Labor: Encourage and incentivize businesses to adopt and enforce ethical labor practices throughout their supply chains. This involves ensuring fair wages, safe working conditions, and non-discriminatory employment practices, thereby creating dignified work and reducing exploitation.
  4. Invest in Accessible Technology for Essential Services: Direct investment towards technological innovations that improve access to quality education and healthcare for underserved communities. This includes e-learning platforms, telehealth solutions, and AI-powered diagnostic tools that can bridge geographical and socioeconomic divides.
  5. Foster Multi-Stakeholder Partnerships: Emphasize collaborative efforts between governments, private sector, civil society, and international organizations. Collective action is essential to address the complex and interconnected challenges of inequality, leveraging diverse expertise and resources for greater impact.
